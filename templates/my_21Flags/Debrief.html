Thank you for participating in this experiment. You have hopefully contributed to some very interesting research.

Please keep the following information to yourself, as sharing it may confound results of people who have not participated in this experiment yet

The game that you have just played, the 21 flags game, is a game of strategy. When played to perfection, a player will be able to backward induct, meaning to reason backward through a series of mapped out game play, in order to reason the correct strategy to win the game. This can even be done from the first move.

What we have tried to observe in this experiment is how well you played based on a treatment you were placed in, and this had any effect on your motivation to win, your perceived understanding of the game, and your overall game play. For example, you were randomly placed in the {% for p in player_treatment %} {% if p.incentivized %} Positive frame of the game. For each win, you received 100 points. How did this gain drive your motivation, perceived understanding and learning as you played the game? {% else %} Negative frame of the game. For each loss, you incurred a loss of 100 points from your initial endowment 0f 1500 points. How did this loss drive your motivation, percieved understanding and learning as you played the game? {% endif %} {% endfor %} Ultimately, after compiling all the data, we hope to see how the effect that you were placed in, compared with the other.

We do hope that you enjoyed this experiment, and better yet, learned something from it. If you would like to lear more, please feel free to reach out to Myself, Daniel Saxon, primary researcher, at daniel.saxon@city.ac.uk or my supervisor, Sergiu Ungureanu at sergiu.ungureanu.2@city.ac.uk.
